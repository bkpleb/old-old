My Reading List (Political Sciences/Econ):

Order from earliest published originally.

- Common Sense And Selected Works [The Rights of Man and The Age of Reason] Of Thomas Paine (1776, 1791, 1794)

- Prices and Production and Other Works - On Money, the Business Cycle, and the Gold Standard by F. A. Hayek (1931)

- The Intelligent Investor by Benjamin Graham (1949)

- Capitalism and Freedom by Milton Friedman (1962)

- The Intellectuals and Socialism By F.A. Hayek (1965)

- Law, Legislation and Liberty by F. A. Hayek (1973)

- Denationalisation of Money: The Argument Refined - An Analysis of the Theory and Practice of Concurrent Currencies (1976)

- Rich Dad Poor Dad by Robert Kiyosaki and Sharon Lechter (1997)

- A History of Political Thought: From Ancient Greece to Early Christianity (Vol. 1) by Janet Coleman (2000)

- A History of Political Thought: From the Middle Ages to the Renaissance (Vol. 2) by Janel Coleman (2000)

- Poor Charlie's Almanack by Charlie Munger (2005)

As someone who loves physical copies of books, I detest e-books. But sometimes you can't help it.

I highly recommend to anyone interested in learning more on libertarian thoughts and anaylsis, read the aviable volumes of numerous works at The Journal of Libertarian Studies provided by the Mises Institute.
